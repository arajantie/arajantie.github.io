Theoretical Physics Euro 2024 Competition

The rules:

  • The basic idea is to buy "shares" in teams. Every time a team wins a match, it earns points. These points are divided among the shareholders according to the number of shares they own.
  • You can buy 8 shares in total. You can choose how you spread them between the teams, but you can buy at most 3 shares in any one team. The number of shares per team is not fixed; the better teams will earn more points, but those points are likely to be spread among more shares. You must decide on your shares before the kick-off of the first match, and it is not possible to change them after that.
  • The number of points a team gets by winning a match depends on the stage of the tournament:
    • 1 for the group stage matches (and 0.5 for a draw)
    • 1 for qualifying to the playoff stage
    • 2 for the round of 16
    • 4 for the quarter finals
    • 8 for the semi finals
    • 16 for the final
        the number of points you get= 
         (the number of points the team got) 
       * (the number of shares you own in the team)
       / (the total number of shares in the team)
  • The winner is the player who has the most points after the tournament. In case of a draw, the player who owns a bigger share in the winning team wins (or if those are equal then the runner-up and so on.)
  • No money will be involved: The winner's prize will be eternal glory. In addition, the losers may feel a moral obligation to buy the winner a beer or some alternative beverage should an opportunity to this arise.

History:

This competition was first run for the 2002 World Cup by Rob Crittenden, Arttu Rajantie and Jochen Weller, who were postdocs at DAMTP in Cambridge at the time. Then the partipants were mainly from Cambridge, but during the years the competition has grown both in numbers and in geographical coverage. The past winners are:
